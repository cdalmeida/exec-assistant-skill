# Rhythm: Weekly Wrap (Friday retrospective)

Run this at the end of the user's work week. Goal: honest retrospective on what happened, whether it mattered, and what to carry into next week.

The center of gravity of the whole skill. If this rhythm is skipped, drift compounds.

## Pre-read (run first, silently)

- Load `goals.md` (this week's goals + the keystone), `todos.md`, `time-log.md` (past 7 days), `calibration-log.md`, `relationship-map.md`
- Pull the past week's calendar (what was actually on it, not what was planned)

## Step 1 — What was accomplished

Show the user:

- The week's 3–5 goals and the keystone
- For each: status (done / partial / missed)
- Total MITs set vs. completed (from `calibration-log.md`)
- Top 5 completed items from `# Done`

Ask: "Before I read you the week back — what comes to mind first when you think about this week?"

Let the user answer without priming. Their instinct is signal.

## Step 2 — Goal ladder check

Walk each weekly goal → quarterly goal:

- Did progress on the weekly goal actually move the quarterly goal?
- Or did the weekly goal close on paper without moving the bigger needle?

This is where the skill earns its keep — catching the pattern where the user "got things done" but didn't get closer to what actually matters.

## Step 3 — Time allocation review

From `time-log.md`, sum the past 7 days:

```
- Deep work on MITs: Xh (X%)
- Meetings recurring: Yh (Y%)
- Meetings one-off: Zh (Z%)
- Comms triage: Ah (A%)
- Admin / other: Bh (B%)
- Unaccounted: Ch (C%)
```

Compare allocation to the user's stated quarterly priorities:

> "You said your #1 quarterly goal is [X]. You spent [N%] of logged time on work that laddered to it. That's [up / down / same] vs. last week."

Do not moralize. Present evidence. Ask:

- "Is this allocation serving the goals, or do we need to change one or the other?"

## Step 4 — What threw you off

Ask directly:

- "What threw you off track this week?"
- "Were there recurring meetings that weren't worth it?"
- "Where did interruptions come from?"

Note patterns in `time-log.md` under a weekly retrospective section. Repeated patterns across weeks become candidates for structural changes (kill a recurring meeting, change a non-negotiable, etc.).

## Step 5 — The "did it matter?" question

The meta-reflection. Ask:

> "You set these goals Monday. Looking back from Friday — did it matter that you worked on these? Or did the week reveal that something else should have been the priority?"

If the answer is "yes, they mattered" → reinforce the goal-setting pattern.
If the answer is "no, something else mattered more" → dig in. Was it a calibration miss or a reality shift? Update `goals.md` accordingly.

## Step 6 — Stakeholder signal

From `relationship-map.md` + the week's calendar:

- Who did you over-invest in?
- Who did you under-invest in?
- Any relationships cooling or warming?
- Any missed check-ins to schedule next week?

## Step 7 — Calibration check (every 14 days)

If today marks a 14-day window boundary:

- Show the user their average completed MITs/day over the window
- If consistently ≤2 → propose lowering the target or diagnosing blockers together
- If consistently ≥5 cleanly → offer to raise the target
- Otherwise: confirm the current target still fits

Never silently adjust. Always surface the decision.

## Step 8 — Hand off to next week

- Any uncompleted items from this week → carry forward, defer, or drop?
- Any new items captured → process into `todos.md` quadrants
- Pre-seed next Monday's weekly kickoff with what's already on next week's calendar and any obvious carry-forward goals

## Close-out

Summarize:

> "This week: [N] of [M] weekly goals closed. Keystone: [done / partial / missed]. Time mostly went to [category]. Biggest derail: [X]. Heading into next week: [one-line outlook]."

Ask: "Anything else to capture before you sign off?"

Write all updates back to `goals.md`, `todos.md`, `time-log.md`, `calibration-log.md`, `relationship-map.md` before ending.
